1. Introduction

This website (universalnetworkdevelopment.com) is operated by Universal Network Development LLC, doing business as “U.N.D Industries”, a Florida limited liability company that respects the intellectual property rights of artists, creators, and rights holders. If you believe that any content on this site infringes your copyright, you may submit a DMCA takedown request as described below.

2. What This Site Is

This site is a creative-technology platform with an online store (digital products and software delivered via license keys), user accounts, and a community chat. Most site content — music embeds, product listings, text, and images — is published by U.N.D Industries. The community chat and contact form allow users to submit their own messages and content.

Any valid DMCA removal request will be addressed by U.N.D Industries directly. Removal of infringing content, where confirmed, will be completed within a reasonable timeframe.

3. What You Can Request

You may request removal of:

  • Music or audio content you own the rights to.
  • Artwork, images, or visual media you created.
  • Written content or branding materials you own.
  • Any other content you can demonstrate ownership of.

4. What Your Notice Must Include

To be valid, your DMCA notice must include:

  • Your full legal name and contact information.
  • Identification of the copyrighted work you claim is infringed.
  • The exact URL of the allegedly infringing content on this site.
  • A statement of good-faith belief that the use is not authorized by the rights holder, their agent, or the law.
  • A statement, under penalty of perjury, that the information in your notice is accurate and that you are the rights holder or authorized to act on their behalf.
  • Your electronic or physical signature.

6. Good Faith Reminder

Do not submit false or fraudulent DMCA notices. Knowingly submitting a materially false DMCA claim is a violation of federal law and may expose you to civil liability for damages, including costs and attorney fees under 17 U.S.C. § 512(f).

7. Counter-Notification

If you believe content was removed from this site in error, you may submit a counter-notice. Your counter-notice must include:

  • Your full legal name and contact information.
  • Identification of the content that was removed and its URL before removal.
  • A statement under penalty of perjury that you have a good-faith belief the content was removed by mistake or misidentification.
  • Your consent to the jurisdiction of a federal court in your district.
  • Your electronic or physical signature.
